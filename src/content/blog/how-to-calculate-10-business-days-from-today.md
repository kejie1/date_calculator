# How to Calculate 10 Business Days From Today

If you are waiting for a bank transfer to clear, tracking an e-commerce shipping delivery, or completing a professional project milestone, you will often need to calculate exactly "10 business days from today". 

## Short Answer
To find 10 business days from today, you skip Saturdays and Sundays as you count forward. A helpful shortcut is to simply **add 14 calendar days** to today's date. Since 10 business days span exactly two complete working weeks, they will always contain exactly 4 weekend days. ## The 14-Day Shortcut Rule

Because standard business weeks contain exactly 5 working days (Monday through Friday) and 2 weekend days (Saturday and Sunday), we can establish a perfect conversion rule:

- **1 week** of business days = **5 working days** (+ 2 weekend days = 7 calendar days)
- **2 weeks** of business days = **10 working days** (+ 4 weekend days = 14 calendar days)

Therefore, if today is **Tuesday, June 16, 2026**:
- Adding 10 business days is mathematically identical to adding exactly 14 calendar days.
- **Target Date:** **Tuesday, June 30, 2026**.

## When the Shortcut Needs Adjustments

The "+14 calendar days" rule works perfectly for any day of the week, with one exception: **when today is a weekend** (Saturday or Sunday). 

- If today is **Saturday**, 10 business days starting counting on the next business Monday. Thus, you add **13 calendar days** to land on the second Friday.
- If today is **Sunday**, you start counting on Monday and add **12 calendar days** to land on that same Friday.

### Does "10 business days" include bank holidays?
No. Standard business day counts are based purely on a five-day week (Monday to Friday) and do not automatically exclude localized public holidays. Always check your regional calendar for holidays.
