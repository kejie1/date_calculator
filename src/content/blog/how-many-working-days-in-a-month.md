# How Many Working Days Are in a Month?

Whether you are calculating salary schedules, estimating monthly project velocity, or planning team resource allocation, knowing the exact number of working days in a month is critical. Because calendar months vary in length and start on different weekdays, this number shifts constantly. If you need to scale this calculation to an entire calendar year, feel free to see our guide on [How to Calculate Working Days in a Year](/blog/how-to-calculate-working-days-in-a-year/).

## Short Answer
On average, a standard calendar month contains **20 to 23 business working days** (excluding Saturdays and Sundays). The exact number depends on the total days in the month (28, 29, 30, or 31) and which day of the week the month begins on. You can use our **Weekdays Calculator** to find the exact workdays for any specific calendar month. If you are calculating shorter business day intervals, check out [How to Calculate Business Days Without Weekends](/blog/how-to-calculate-business-days-without-weekends/).

## Standard Working Days Distribution

Here is a general guide to how many working days (Monday-Friday) typically occur inside months of various lengths:

### 1. February (28 Days)
- If starting on a Monday: **20 business days** (4 perfect weeks).
- Any other start weekday: **20 business days** (exactly 8 weekend days and 20 workdays).

### 2. February (29 Days - Leap Year)
- Can contain **20 or 21 business days**, depending on the starting weekday.

### 3. 30-Day Months (April, June, September, November)
- Usually contain **20 to 22 business days**.
- If the month starts on a Saturday or Sunday, it will have more weekend days, lowering the business day count.

### 4. 31-Day Months (Jan, Mar, May, Jul, Aug, Oct, Dec)
- Standardly contain **21 to 23 business days**.
- To achieve 23 business days, the month must start on a weekday (e.g. Monday, Tuesday, or Wednesday), minimizing the weekend overlap.

## Practical Calculation Example

Let's look at the exact breakdown of working days for **June 2026**:

- June has **30 calendar days**.
- In 2026, June 1st falls on a **Monday**.
- Since June starts on a Monday, let's map the week cycles:
  - Week 1: June 1 (Mon) – June 7 (Sun) $\rightarrow$ **5 workdays, 2 weekend days**
  - Week 2: June 8 (Mon) – June 14 (Sun) $\rightarrow$ **5 workdays, 2 weekend days**
  - Week 3: June 15 (Mon) – June 21 (Sun) $\rightarrow$ **5 workdays, 2 weekend days**
  - Week 4: June 22 (Mon) – June 28 (Sun) $\rightarrow$ **5 workdays, 2 weekend days**
  - Remaining: June 29 (Mon) and June 30 (Tue) $\rightarrow$ **2 workdays**
- **Totals:**
  $$4 \text{ full weeks} \times 5 \text{ days} + 2 \text{ remaining days} = 22 \text{ business working days}$$
  And there are exactly **8 weekend days** in June 2026.

## FAQ

### Does this include public holidays?
No. Standard monthly business day figures are usually computed based purely on standard Mon-Fri weeks. Public holidays like New Year's Day, Memorial Day, or Christmas must be subtracted manually based on your country.

### Which month typically has the fewest working days?
February always contains the fewest working days, with exactly 20 workdays in standard years (and occasionally 21 in leap years).

### Can I run this analysis for an entire year?
Yes. An average calendar year contains exactly **260 to 262 business working days**, depending on leap year alignments. Use our tools to count any year's precise distribution.
